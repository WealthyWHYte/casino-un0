# BRUTAL CEO OBJECTIONS: What They'll Really Say
## Preparing for the Harsh Reality of Executive Pushback

---

## THE HARSH TRUTH

**You're asking for $3M + 9% + 30% + 9% equity from executives who've built billion-dollar empires. They didn't get there by giving away massive percentages to employees with ideas.**

---

## JIM ALLEN'S BRUTAL OBJECTIONS

### **"Who the hell are you?"**
*"You work at one of our properties and you want $3 million? I've been in gaming for 40 years. What makes you think you deserve more than our senior VPs who've been here for decades?"*

**Your Reality Check:** You're a casino employee asking for CEO-level compensation. They'll see you as delusional.

### **"We can do this without you"**
*"Thanks for the idea, but we have a business development team. We can call Mattel ourselves. Why would we pay you millions for a phone call we can make?"*

**Your Reality Check:** Once you give them the idea, they can cut you out completely.

### **"This is our intellectual property now"**
*"You developed this concept while employed by Seminole. Any gaming innovations belong to the company. You should be grateful we don't fire you for wasting time on side projects."*

**Your Reality Check:** Employment contracts often claim ownership of employee innovations.

### **"9% of revenue? Are you insane?"**
*"Our senior executives don't get 9% of revenue. Our board members don't get 9% of revenue. You want more than people who've invested hundreds of millions?"*

**Your Reality Check:** 9% of $50M is $4.5M annually - more than most C-suite executives make.

---

## MATTEL'S BRUTAL OBJECTIONS

### **"We don't need a middleman"**
*"Seminole can contact us directly. Why would we pay licensing fees to someone who doesn't own our IP? You're just a casino employee with an idea."*

**Your Reality Check:** Mattel has no incentive to include you in their licensing deal.

### **"Prove the market demand first"**
*"Come back when you have actual gaming revenue data. We're not licensing our brand based on PowerPoint presentations and wishful thinking."*

**Your Reality Check:** They want proven results, not projections.

### **"Our licensing fees are our business"**
*"Why would we share our licensing revenue with you? That's between us and Seminole. You're not a party to our agreement."*

**Your Reality Check:** You have no legal standing in their licensing negotiations.

---

## SEMINOLE TRIBAL COUNCIL OBJECTIONS

### **"This benefits you more than the tribe"**
*"You're asking for generational wealth while our tribal members get what? This looks like you're exploiting our gaming compact for personal gain."*

**Your Reality Check:** Tribal gaming is about tribal sovereignty and member benefits, not enriching employees.

### **"We have fiduciary duties"**
*"We're responsible to 4,000+ tribal members. How do we justify giving one employee millions while tribal programs need funding?"*

**Your Reality Check:** They have legal obligations to prioritize tribal interests over individual employees.

---

## LEGAL TEAM OBJECTIONS

### **"This creates massive liability"**
*"If we give one employee 9% of gaming revenue, every employee with an idea will demand the same. This sets a dangerous precedent that could cost us hundreds of millions."*

**Your Reality Check:** Legal teams exist to minimize risk and liability.

### **"Employment law issues"**
*"This could be seen as preferential treatment, creating discrimination claims from other employees. The NLRB could investigate our employment practices."*

**Your Reality Check:** Large payouts to individual employees create legal exposure.

### **"Gaming regulation concerns"**
*"Gaming commissions scrutinize all revenue-sharing agreements. This could trigger regulatory investigations into our licensing and employment practices."*

**Your Reality Check:** Gaming is heavily regulated, and unusual compensation arrangements draw scrutiny.

---

## FINANCIAL TEAM OBJECTIONS

### **"The numbers don't work"**
*"$3M finder's fee plus 9% of revenue plus 30% of licensing fees? That's potentially $10M+ annually. We could hire 50 gaming executives for that cost."*

**Your Reality Check:** CFOs focus on cost-benefit analysis, and your ask is expensive.

### **"ROI doesn't justify the cost"**
*"Even if UNO generates $50M annually, paying you $4.5M means we need $60M+ just to break even on your compensation. The math doesn't work."*

**Your Reality Check:** They'll calculate whether your compensation exceeds the value you create.

### **"Opportunity cost analysis"**
*"For $3M upfront, we could develop 10 other gaming concepts internally. Why put all our eggs in one basket with one employee?"*

**Your Reality Check:** They have multiple investment options for that capital.

---

## OPERATIONAL OBJECTIONS

### **"Implementation risk"**
*"What happens when Mattel says no? What happens when players don't like it? What happens when it fails? You get paid regardless, but we lose millions."*

**Your Reality Check:** You're asking for guaranteed payment while they take all the risk.

### **"You have no gaming development experience"**
*"Have you ever launched a casino game? Do you understand gaming regulations? Have you managed a $50M product rollout? Why should we trust you with this?"*

**Your Reality Check:** They'll question your qualifications to execute what you're proposing.

### **"Staffing and training costs"**
*"New games require dealer training, floor supervision, marketing campaigns, and ongoing support. Those costs could exceed your projected revenues."*

**Your Reality Check:** Implementation costs money, and they'll want detailed budgets.

---

## COMPETITIVE OBJECTIONS

### **"First-mover advantage is temporary"**
*"Even if UNO works, competitors will copy it within months. Our advantage disappears quickly, but your payments continue forever."*

**Your Reality Check:** Gaming innovations are quickly replicated by competitors.

### **"Market saturation concerns"**
*"How many people actually want to play UNO in a casino? The novelty might wear off in six months, leaving us with expensive tables nobody uses."*

**Your Reality Check:** They'll question long-term market demand.

## HOW TO FIGHT BACK

### **Document Everything**
- Email trails showing your initiative
- Timestamped research and analysis
- Proof of concept development
- All communications with management

### **Legal Protection First**
- Hire gaming attorney BEFORE meeting
- File intellectual property claims
- Establish independent contractor status
- Create paper trail of value creation

### **Leverage Your Position**
- You work there - you understand operations
- You have the Mattel connection strategy
- You've done the market research
- You can walk away and take it elsewhere

### **Alternative Strategies**
- License the concept to competitors
- Develop it independently
- Partner with other gaming companies
- Create your own gaming consulting firm

---

## REALISTIC NEGOTIATION STRATEGY

### **Phase 1: Establish Credibility**
- Present comprehensive business case
- Show detailed implementation plan
- Demonstrate market research depth
- Prove you're serious, not just dreaming

### **Phase 2: Start High, Negotiate Down**
- Open with aggressive terms
- Expect massive pushback
- Have fallback positions ready
- Know your walk-away point

### **Phase 3: Secure Something**
- Get written agreement for ANY compensation
- Establish precedent for revenue sharing
- Protect against being cut out
- Build foundation for future negotiations

## BOTTOM LINE TRUTH

**They're going to try to lowball you, steal your idea, or cut you out entirely. That's what billion-dollar companies do to employees with ideas.**

**Your job is to:**
1. **Protect yourself legally** before revealing everything
2. **Document your contribution** so they can't claim it's theirs
3. **Have alternatives ready** if they try to screw you
4. **Negotiate from strength**, not desperation
5. **Be prepared to walk away** if the deal isn't fair

**Tuesday's meeting isn't about getting your dream deal - it's about not getting completely screwed while establishing your value in this opportunity.**

**Fight for every dollar, because they sure as hell won't give you anything out of kindness.**
