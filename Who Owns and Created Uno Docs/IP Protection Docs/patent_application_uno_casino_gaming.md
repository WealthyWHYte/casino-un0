## BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION

Traditional casino table games have remained largely unchanged for decades, with blackjack, poker, baccarat, and roulette dominating casino floors. While these games have proven successful, there exists a significant opportunity to attract new demographics, particularly younger players who seek more engaging and familiar gaming experiences.

The card game UNO, created by Merle Robbins in 1971 and currently owned by Mattel, Inc., has achieved global recognition with over 150 million units sold worldwide. UNO's simple rules, social interaction elements, and universal appeal make it an ideal candidate for adaptation into a casino gaming format.

Current casino gaming lacks:
1. Games that appeal to millennials and Generation Z demographics
2. Social interaction elements that encourage group play
3. Familiar intellectual properties that reduce learning curves
4. Progressive betting structures that maintain player engagement
5. Tournament formats suitable for competitive play

Prior art in casino gaming includes various attempts to adapt popular games for casino use, but none have successfully combined the specific elements of UNO's gameplay with sophisticated casino betting mechanics and anti-counting security measures.

### Betting Structure and Game Mechanics

**Phase 1: Ante and Initial Deal**
1. All players place mandatory ante bet ($5-$100 range)
2. Each player receives 7 cards
3. Top card of deck is turned face up to start discard pile
4. Progressive jackpot contributions collected (10% of ante bets)

**Phase 2: Primary Gameplay**
1. Players take turns matching color, number, or action
2. Action cards trigger special betting opportunities:
   - Skip: Next player loses turn, betting continues
   - Reverse: Direction changes, new betting round
   - Draw Two: Target player draws cards, side bet opportunities
   - Wild: Player chooses color, color bonus bets activated

**Phase 3: Strategic Betting Rounds**
1. After each action card played, betting round occurs
2. Players may fold, call, or raise based on hand strength
3. Pot accumulates throughout gameplay
4. Side bets resolved based on specific outcomes

**Phase 4: Resolution and Payout**
1. First player to empty hand wins primary pot
2. Remaining players ranked by cards remaining
3. Side bets paid according to established odds
4. Progressive jackpot triggered by specific conditions
5. New hand begins with increased ante structure
